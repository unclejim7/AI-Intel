THOMSON REUTERS DEFINES "FIDUCIARY-GRADE AI" — AND DRAWS A LINE IN THE SAND

On May 27, Thomson Reuters published its formal standard for Fiduciary-Grade AI™, a four-principle framework declaring that AI used in high-stakes professional contexts — legal, tax, audit, compliance — must be grounded in authoritative domain-specific content, protected by rigorous privacy safeguards, shaped by subject-matter experts, and built to produce transparent, verifiable outputs. The announcement, timed two days ahead of AICPA ENGAGE season, is a direct shot across the bow at general-purpose AI tools encroaching on professional services territory. For practitioners, the standard formalizes what many have intuitively required: every material AI output must be traceable to a source a CPA can independently locate, cite, and defend to a regulator. For anyone building AI products in this space, Thomson Reuters just handed you a benchmark — and a challenge: can you meet it, or are you a GPT wrapper in a suit?

Reporter's Take Thomson Reuters isn't just publishing a standard — they're writing the product requirements document for every credible AI vendor in accounting, which means firms evaluating tools now have a checklist competitors must answer for.

◼ The Agentic Corner
Campfire — Ember Agents for Finance Close

Campfire launched Ember Agents in March 2026 — a system of AI workers embedded in the financial close process. The platform runs two agent types: continuous agents that operate 24/7 on recurring operational work (transaction matching, AP/AR processing, anomaly detection, accruals management), and on-demand agents that execute scheduled tasks like period-over-period flux analysis and board reporting on a monthly close checklist. Every agent action is logged, sourced to underlying GL data, and reviewable before posting; confidence thresholds let finance teams dial exactly how much the system acts autonomously versus surfaces for human review. Campfire is positioned as a vertical AI platform for CFO-level finance teams, not CPA firm tax workflows, but the architecture — auditable agent logs, confidence scoring, human-in-loop escalation — maps directly to the design pattern the tax space is converging on.

Strategic Read The continuous vs. on-demand agent architecture Campfire deployed is the template worth studying for tax firm product design: routine compliance work runs autonomously, deadline-triggered deliverables run on schedule, and nothing posts without a traceable log — that's the accountability layer regulators will eventually require.
