XeroForce
By: Xero (ASX: XRO)
What it does
A no-code, natural language AI agent builder that lets accountants and small businesses create custom, always-on workflows by describing them in plain English — connecting Xero's core platform to third-party apps and automating everything from month-end close to payrun approvals and tax document organization. Agents run in the background over days or weeks, waiting on events like email replies or filing deadlines, and can operate at practice-wide scale across hundreds of clients simultaneously.
Built for
Xero-native accounting firms and bookkeepers who want firm-specific automation without hiring a developer; also available to small business owners managing their own books on Xero.
CPA use case
A firm with 200 QuickBooks-migrated clients on Xero could describe an agent: "Every month-end, pull the P&L for each client, flag any account with variance >15% vs. prior period, and draft a client email summarizing exceptions." XeroForce executes that for all 200 clients automatically, with a full audit trail of every action taken.
Pricing
Currently in invite-only alpha; general release pricing not yet announced. Xero's standard plans begin around $20–$47/month; XeroForce pricing will likely be a tier or add-on.
Key edge
Unlike generic AI platforms, every XeroForce agent action is fully logged and traceable — critical for professional compliance. The domain-specific financial logic is baked in rather than bolted on, which distinguishes it from generic workflow tools like Zapier or Make.
Verdict: The most practically significant agent-builder announcement for accountants this month — if you're Xero-native, get on the alpha waitlist immediately; if you're on QuickBooks, this is a serious argument for reconsidering your stack.

◼ The Agentic Corner
Paychex WISE — The Incumbent Agentic Playbook
Paychex WISE (Workforce Intelligence Strengthened by Expertise) is architecturally notable because it doesn't deploy a single AI agent — it deploys a coordinated digital workforce of agents running across Flex, Paycor, and SurePayroll simultaneously, each operating within a defined function: Intelligence (predictive analytics and HR reporting), Assistants (multi-channel task support via chat, voice, email, SMS), and Advisory (proactive alerts to human Paychex experts who then guide clients through complex decisions). The system is grounded in five decades of payroll and HR data from ~800,000 clients — a proprietary corpus no startup can replicate. This is the "AI augments the expert advisor" model, not the "AI replaces the advisor" model, which is strategically important: Paychex keeps its human relationship layer while automating the workflow underneath it.
Strategic Read: Paychex WISE is the clearest proof yet that incumbents with deep proprietary data are using it to build agentic moats — for founders building in tax/accounting AI, "what data do we uniquely control?" is now the most important product strategy question.
